Keep the deck you already have. By embedding your Google Slides presentation into AhaSlides, you can layer in polls, quizzes, and word clouds around your existing content — no need to rebuild anything from scratch. Your slides stay in Google Slides; AhaSlides wraps them in an interactive experience.

## Step 1 — Publish Your Google Slides Deck to the Web

Before you can embed your deck, you need to generate a public link from Google Slides.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the publish dialog">
    In Google Slides, click **File → Share → Publish to web**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the presentation">
    In the dialog that appears, click **Publish** and confirm when prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the link">
    Copy the published link that Google Slides generates. You'll paste this into AhaSlides in the next step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Publishing to the web makes your slides viewable by anyone with the link. If your deck contains confidential content, consider whether this is appropriate before proceeding.
</Note>

## Step 2 — Embed the Deck in AhaSlides

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a Google Slides slide">
    In AhaSlides, add a new slide and select the **Google Slides** slide type from the slide picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the published link">
    Paste the link you copied from Google Slides into the **Google Slides' Published link** field. Your deck loads as an embedded frame, ready to present inside AhaSlides.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Navigating Your Deck During a Presentation

While presenting, the embedded Google Slides frame behaves like a mini version of Google Slides. Use the **navigation arrows in the bottom-left corner** of the frame to move forward and backward through your slides.

You can also access the full Google Slides toolbar by clicking the **three-dot menu** in the bottom-left corner of the frame. This gives you access to:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Speaker Notes" icon="note-sticky">
    View your presenter notes for each slide without the audience seeing them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Laser Pointer & Pen" icon="pen">
    Draw attention to specific parts of a slide with the laser pointer or annotate directly on screen.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Full-Screen Mode" icon="expand">
    Expand the Google Slides frame to fill the screen for a cleaner presentation view.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto-Play" icon="play">
    Automatically advance through your slides at a set interval — useful for kiosk-style displays.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Adding Interactive Slides Around Your Content

The real power of this integration is mixing your Google Slides content with AhaSlides interactive slides in a single, flowing presentation.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a new slide">
    In the slide panel on the left, click the **+** button to add a new AhaSlides slide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an interactive type">
    Pick from polls, quizzes, word clouds, Q&A, rating scales, and more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Position it in your flow">
    Drag the new slide to the right position — before your Google Slides section, after it, or between chunks of content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  A common pattern is: a Google Slides content section → a live poll to check understanding → more slides → a quiz at the end. Mixing the two types keeps audiences engaged throughout and lets you gather feedback at natural breakpoints.
</Tip>
