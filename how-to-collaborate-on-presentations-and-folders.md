# How to collaborate on presentations and folders

Collaborative editing lets you work on presentations or folders with other AhaSlides users, whether they are inside your team or outside it.

{% hint style="info" %}
Collaborating with members of your team plan is available on all plans. See [Managing your team members](managing-your-team-members.md). Collaborating with individuals outside your team is supported on Pro, Edu Large, and Enterprise plans, though all users can use external collaboration during the current promotional offer.
{% endhint %}

{% hint style="warning" %}
Features of the owner's plan are not shared through collaboration. If you need a paid plan for multiple users, see [Purchasing multiple licenses for AhaSlides Team plan subscription](purchasing-multiple-licenses-for-ahaslides-team-plan-subscription.md).
{% endhint %}

## Adding collaborators to a presentation

There are two ways to add collaborators to a presentation:

- In the presentation editor toolbar, click the icon that shows your profile image and a **+** icon.
- Click the **Share** menu button and choose the **Add Collaborators** tab.

Either option opens the invitation menu. Enter your collaborators' email addresses and choose the **Editor** or **Presenter** role for each one.

{% hint style="info" %}
For more on the Presenter role, see [Multiple presenters, one presentation with the Presenter role](presenter-role.md).
{% endhint %}

Once added, collaborators are listed in this menu alongside a dropdown where you can remove them. Each collaborator receives an email telling them they have editing rights in your presentation. You always remain the owner.

### Inviting editors via link

You can also invite Editors using **Invite editors via link** in the **Share** menu. This option does not support the Presenter role.

1. Turn on the **Invite editors via link** toggle.
2. A shareable link appears. Click **Copy** to copy it.
3. Send the link to your collaborators.

When recipients open the link, they are prompted to sign in if they are not already. After signing in, they gain access to your presentation as an Editor.

## Adding collaborators to a folder

1. From the **My Presentations** view, locate and select the folder you want to share.
2. Click the **3-dot button** to the right of the folder, or right-click the folder, and choose **Add Collaborators**.
3. Type the collaborators' email addresses, select a role, then click **Add** to grant access.

The chosen collaborators then have access to the shared folder.

## Editing and viewing together

It is not yet possible for two people to edit a presentation at the same time. Collaborators can edit and present the same presentation, but only one person can actively edit or present at any given time.

Collaborators can view the presentation while it is being edited. When a collaborator is viewing the same presentation, their profile picture appears in the top toolbar beside the **Invite** button.

When one person is editing and another is viewing, the viewer sees a bar at the top of the editor with two options:

1. **Take over**: pressing this immediately makes you the editor and turns the current editor into a viewer.
2. **Refresh**: pressing this refreshes the page so you can view the latest changes made by the editor.

As long as no one is currently editing or presenting, any collaborator can press **Present** to start presenting immediately, which takes over the presentation.

## Finding shared presentations or folders

As the owner, any presentation or folder you share still appears in your **My Presentations** tab alongside everything else you created.

As a collaborator, any presentation or folder you were invited to appears in the **Shared with Me** tab. To leave a presentation or folder you were invited to, right-click it or click its 3-dot button, then choose **Leave**.

## Moving presentations across folders

{% hint style="info" %}
You can move a presentation to a folder only if you have edit access to all three of the following: the source folder, the presentation, and the destination folder. To let collaborators move your presentations, move the presentations into a folder and invite the collaborators to that folder as Editor. Only presentations you own can be moved to the root folder of your My Presentations page.
{% endhint %}

1. Click the checkbox next to the thumbnail of the presentation. You can select multiple presentations, and the checkbox appears only for presentations you are able to move. Then click **Move to** on the toolbar at the bottom of the page. You can also right-click a presentation, or open its option menu, to find **Move to**.
2. Choose the destination folder and click **Move**.
3. The result depends on the folders involved. Moving a presentation from one private folder to another private folder happens immediately. In all other cases, a message about changing access rights appears first; click **Move** to proceed, and the presentation moves to the destination folder.

## Frequently asked questions

### How do I share an AhaSlides presentation with someone else to edit?

In the editor toolbar, click the icon with your profile image and a **+**, or open the **Share** menu and choose **Add Collaborators**. Enter your collaborators' email addresses and give each one the **Editor** role.

### Can two people edit the same AhaSlides presentation at the same time?

No. Collaborators can edit and present the same presentation, but only one person can actively edit or present at any given time. A viewer can press **Take over** to become the editor.

### How do I invite editors with a link?

In the **Share** menu, turn on the **Invite editors via link** toggle, then click **Copy** and send the link. Recipients sign in and gain Editor access. This option does not support the Presenter role.

### Where do I find presentations shared with me?

Any presentation or folder you were invited to appears in the **Shared with Me** tab. To leave one, right-click it or click its 3-dot button, then choose **Leave**.

### Which plans support collaborating with people outside my team?

External collaboration is supported on Pro, Edu Large, and Enterprise plans, though all users can use it during the current promotional offer. Collaborating with members of your own team plan is available on all plans.
