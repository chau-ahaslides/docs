AhaSlides gives you a full set of tools to control how your audience joins and experiences your presentation. Before you go live, you can configure everything from the join link to the profanity filter, audio tracks, and presenter notes. This page walks through every presenting setting available.

## Sharing your presentation with participants

To get your audience responding to your slides, share one of the following with them:

- A **URL / access link** they can type into any browser
- An **access code** they can enter at [audience.ahaslides.com](http://audience.ahaslides.com/)
- A **QR code** they can scan with their phone camera

### Sharing the URL or access code

The URL and access code appear in the joining instruction bar at the top of every slide, both in the editor and during a live presentation. The access code is the short string that follows `ahaslides.com/` in the URL.

You can also find and copy the link from the **Share** menu → **Invite Audience** tab.

<Tip>
  During a live presentation, click the URL access link to copy it to your clipboard instantly so you can paste it into a chat or email.
</Tip>

#### Customising your access code

<Note>
  Custom access codes require a **Pro plan**.
</Note>

In the **Share** menu → **Invite Audience** tab, click the code in the box to edit it. A custom code must be unique, under 10 characters, and contain only letters and numbers. Click **Save** when you are done.

#### Permanent access link

Your presentation also has a permanent long-form URL that never changes, even when you update the short code. In the **Share** menu, click the **three-dot** icon next to **Copy** to reveal the **Copy permanent link** option.

### Sharing the QR code

Each presentation has one unique QR code that never changes.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Side column while presenting" icon="qrcode">
    Click anywhere in the top bar during your presentation (or press **Q** on your keyboard) to reveal a QR code column on the left. It stays visible until you close it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="QR Code slide" icon="display">
    Add a dedicated QR Code slide to your presentation. When you reach it, the QR code appears on screen for your audience to scan.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

You can also download a PNG of your QR code from the **Share** menu → **Invite Audience** tab → **Download**.

## Self-paced mode

Self-paced mode lets your audience move through the presentation at their own speed without a live host. This is ideal for homework assignments, surveys, and asynchronous training.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    In the presentation editor, click the **Settings** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Who takes the lead">
    Find and click the **Who takes the lead** option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Audience (self-paced)">
    Select **Audience (self-paced)**. Your participants can now navigate through the slides using the **Back** and **Next** buttons on their devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Set your presentation to **public mode** before sharing the link with participants, so they can join without a passcode.
</Tip>

## Preview and test mode

Preview mode lets you see and interact with your slides exactly as your audience would, before you go live.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Preview mode">
    In the presentation editor, click the grey **Preview** button in the top bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test your interactions">
    You see two screens side by side: the **presenter screen** on the left and the **participant screen** on the right. Use the participant screen to interact with each slide and confirm everything works as expected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate through slides">
    Use the arrows beside the presenter screen or the **left and right arrow keys** on your keyboard to move through your slides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the editor">
    When you are finished testing, click **Back to Editor** in the top-left corner.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Profanity filter

The profanity filter blocks swear words submitted by participants on any slide where they type freely. Enable it once and it applies across your whole presentation.

The filter works on:
- Word cloud slides
- Open-ended slides
- Q&A slides
- Short answer quiz slides
- Participant name fields

Blocked words appear as a series of asterisks matching the number of letters in the word.

**Three places to toggle the filter:**

1. **Settings tab** in the editor
2. **Content tab** of any Open-Ended, Q&A, Brainstorm, or Word Cloud slide
3. **Menu icon** while presenting → **Filter profanity**

<Note>
  The profanity filter currently covers the vast majority of profanities in English. To report an error or suggest an improvement, contact [hi@ahaslides.com](mailto:hi@ahaslides.com).
</Note>

## Emoji reactions

Reactions let your audience respond in the moment without interrupting the flow of your presentation. You choose which reactions are available.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    While editing your presentation, open the **Settings** menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Other settings">
    Click **Other settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your reactions">
    Choose which reactions participants can send: **Like**, **Love**, **Haha**, **Wow**, and **Sad**. Only the reactions you enable appear on participants' devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Audience get-slides button

The get-slides button is a small banner that appears on participants' devices when you reach the final slide. It invites your audience to download your slides.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click **Settings** in the top toolbar of the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Other settings">
    Click **Other settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable or disable the button">
    Check **Allow audience to download the slides** to show the button, or uncheck it to hide it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  To review each download request before it goes through, check the **Require my approval** box under **Allow audience to download the slides**.
</Note>

## Slide and presentation audio

Add music or audio to individual slides or across your entire presentation to create a more immersive experience.

<Note>
  Slide audio and Presentation audio are available on **paid plans** only. Sound effects are available on all plans.
</Note>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Slide audio">
    Slide audio plays on a specific slide and can also play through participants' devices.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Select a slide">
        Select the slide you want to add audio to, then click the **Audio** section in the right-hand panel.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Choose or upload a track">
        Under the **Slide** tab, select a song from the AhaSlides library, or click **Upload** to add your own MP3 file (maximum 15 MB).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Configure playback settings">
        Set your preferred playback options:
        - **Autoplay** — plays immediately when the slide appears
        - **On repeat** — loops the audio automatically
        - **Playable on audience's devices** — plays through participants' devices as well; enabling this also reveals a **Show media controls** option so participants can control playback themselves
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Presentation audio">
    Presentation audio plays continuously across the whole presentation on the presenter's device only. It pauses automatically on any slide that has its own Slide audio.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Select any slide">
        Click any slide, then open the **Audio** section in the right-hand panel.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Open the Presentation tab">
        Click the **Presentation** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select a track">
        Choose a song from the dropdown. Note that you cannot upload a custom audio file for Presentation audio.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Sound effects">
    Sound effects provide audio feedback on the presenter screen and participants' devices at key moments (answer reveals, countdowns, leaderboards, and more).

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Audio settings">
        Click any slide, open the **Audio** section, and go to the **Presentation** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Toggle sound effects">
        Under **Sound effects**, use the toggles next to **On the presenting screen** and **On participants' devices** to enable or disable them independently.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Presenter notes

Write private notes for each slide. Your audience never sees them — they appear only on your own screen through Remote control.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add notes in the editor">
    In the editor, find the notes box below each slide preview. Type your notes there. They save automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View notes while presenting">
    Start your presentation using **Remote control**. The Remote control window opens on your screen and displays the notes for each slide as you advance through the presentation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Notes are visible only in the Remote control window. They never appear on the projected presentation or on participants' devices.
</Note>

## Moving and editing multiple slides

You can rearrange, edit, duplicate, and delete multiple slides at once from the navigation panel or using Grid View.

### Selecting multiple slides

- **CTRL + click** to select individual slides
- **SHIFT + click** to select a range of slides

### Grid View

Grid View shows all your slides in a grid, making it easier to work with large presentations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Grid View">
    Click **Grid View** at the bottom of the left-hand panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select slides">
    Click the **Select** button, click the slides you want, then click **Done**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the action panel">
    With slides selected, the bottom panel gives you options to:
    - **Rearrange** — move selected slides to a new position
    - **Duplicate** — create copies of the selected slides
    - **Copy to** — copy slides into a different presentation
    - **Clear result** — erase audience response data from selected slides
    - **Delete** — remove selected slides permanently
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit backgrounds or audio for multiple slides">
    With slides selected, use the right-hand **Design** tab to edit colours and background images, or the **Audio** tab to add a track across multiple slides simultaneously.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Display language

AhaSlides has two independent language settings: one for your presenter interface and one for what your audience sees on their devices.

<Accordion title="Changing the presenter interface language">
  The presenter interface language affects your menus, buttons, and labels. It applies to your entire account.

  1. Log in and open the dashboard.
  2. Click the **globe icon** in the top-right corner.
  3. Select your preferred language from the dropdown. The interface switches instantly.

  The presenter interface is available in **27 languages**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Changing the audience display language">
  The audience display language controls what participants see on their devices — questions, answer options, and the join screen. This setting is per presentation.

  1. Open the presentation in the editor.
  2. Click the **Settings** icon in the right-hand panel.
  3. Click **Presentation language**.
  4. Select your audience's language from the dropdown.

  The audience display is available in **32 languages**.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Because the two language settings are independent, you can keep your presenter interface in English while your audience sees the presentation in French, Spanish, Japanese, or any other supported language.
</Tip>
