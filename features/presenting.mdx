When you're ready to go live, AhaSlides gives you several ways to share your presentation with an audience and a set of presenter controls to manage the session. This page covers everything from sharing options to audio, display language, and managing slides during a live session.

## Sharing Your Presentation

Participants join your session at [audience.ahaslides.com](https://audience.ahaslides.com) using an access code or a direct URL. No app download is required — the audience experience runs entirely in a mobile browser.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="URL" icon="link">
    A direct link your audience can click to join instantly — useful for virtual events, emails, and chat messages.
  </Card>
  <Card title="QR Code" icon="qrcode">
    Display the QR code on your presentation screen for in-person events. Participants scan it with their phone camera to join immediately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Access Code" icon="key">
    A short alphanumeric code participants type at audience.ahaslides.com. Easy to read aloud to a room.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custom Code" icon="star">
    **Pro plan.** Replace the auto-generated access code with your own branded shortcode — for example, your company name or event hashtag.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  **Permanent link — Pro plan.** Pro plan users can enable a permanent link so the same URL and access code persist across multiple sessions of the same presentation. Participants can bookmark it once and use it every time.
</Note>

## Self-Paced Mode

Normally, you control slide progression for everyone in the room — when you advance, everyone's screen advances. In **Self-Paced Mode**, each participant moves through the slides at their own speed.

This is ideal for:
- Asynchronous surveys shared via link
- Training modules participants complete on their own schedule
- Situations where you're not controlling a shared screen

Enable Self-Paced Mode in **Presentation Settings** before starting your session. Once enabled, participants see navigation arrows on their device to move forward and back through slides independently.

## Preview / Test Mode

Before going live, use **Preview Mode** to experience your presentation exactly as a participant would — without recording any real response data.

Click the **Preview** button (eye icon 👁) next to the **Present** button in the editor. Your browser opens a presenter view while a second tab simulates the participant view. Close the preview tab when you're done; no data is saved.

## Profanity Filter

AhaSlides includes a built-in profanity filter for text input from your audience. It applies to:

- Word Cloud slides
- Open Ended slides
- Q&A slides
- Type Answer slides
- Participant name fields

Enable the profanity filter per-slide in the slide settings panel, or switch it on globally for all applicable slides in **Presentation Settings**.

## Emoji Reactions

During a live session, participants can send emoji reactions — 👍 ❤️ 😂 😮 😠 — that float across the presenter screen and participant screens in real time.

Enable or disable emoji reactions in **Presentation Settings** before or during your session. Turning them off removes the reaction toolbar from participants' devices.

## Audience Controls

**Get Slides button:** Optionally allow participants to download a static copy of your presentation after the session ends. Toggle this on in **Presentation Settings → Audience Controls**. When enabled, a **Get Slides** button appears on participants' screens at the end of the session.

## Slide Audio

<Note>
  Audio features require a paid plan (Essential, Pro, or Enterprise).
</Note>

AhaSlides supports three types of audio in a presentation:

- **Slide Audio:** Attach an audio clip to an individual slide. It plays automatically when that slide is shown during the session. Configure it per-slide in the slide settings panel.
- **Presentation Audio:** Set background music that plays throughout the entire session. Upload a track in **Presentation Settings → Audio**.
- **Sound Effects:** Enable quiz sound effects — countdown ticking, correct answer chime, incorrect answer buzz — on Quiz slides. Toggle these on in the Quiz slide settings.

## Presenter Notes

Write notes for each slide in the **Notes** panel at the bottom of the editor. Your notes are not visible to participants at any point during the session.

During presenting, access your notes by opening the **Remote Control** view on a separate device (phone or tablet). Navigate to your presentation's remote control URL, log in, and you'll see your current slide, your notes, and navigation controls — all from your second screen.

## Managing Slides During Presenting

**Navigating:** Click the **Next** and **Previous** buttons on the presenter toolbar, or use the **← →** keyboard arrow keys to advance or go back between slides.

**Grid View:** Press **G** on your keyboard to open a grid overview of all slides. Click any slide thumbnail to jump directly to it — useful for skipping ahead or returning to an earlier slide mid-session.

**Multi-selecting slides in the editor:** In the slide panel, hold **Ctrl** (Windows) or **Cmd** (Mac) and click to select individual slides, or hold **Shift** and click to select a range. Move or delete multiple slides at once to reorganise your deck quickly.

## Display Language

AhaSlides supports **27 presenter languages** and **32 audience languages**, so you can run sessions in your local language regardless of where your team or audience is based.

- **Presenter language:** Set in your **Account Settings**. Controls the language of the AhaSlides editor and presenter toolbar.
- **Audience language:** Set per-presentation in **Presentation Settings**. Controls the language of the AhaSlides UI shown on participants' phones and browsers.

<Tip>
  The audience language setting controls the language of AhaSlides interface text — buttons, labels, and prompts — shown to participants. It does **not** translate the content of your slides (your questions, answer options, or images).
</Tip>
