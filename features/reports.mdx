After your presentation, AhaSlides automatically compiles a report of everything your audience submitted. You can review overall participation metrics, drill into each participant's individual responses, export the full dataset to Excel, and reset results so the presentation is ready for your next audience.

---

## The presentation report

The presentation report gives you a high-level summary of how your audience engaged with your slides. It is available as soon as at least one person has interacted with your presentation.

### How to find your report

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="From the editor" icon="chart-bar">
    Click the **Report** button in the top-right toolbar of the presentation editor.
  </Card>
  <Card title="From the dashboard" icon="house">
    On the **My Presentations** dashboard, hover over a presentation and click the purple **Report** button.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### What the report includes

**Key metrics at a glance:**

| Metric | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Total Participants | Number of participants who joined |
| Total Reactions | Total emoji reactions from the audience |
| Average Submissions | Average submissions per participant across polls, word clouds, scales, open-ended, brainstorm, and Q&A |
| Accuracy | Percentage of correct answers on quiz questions |
| Rating | Average rating given by participants |

**Participants column** — a detailed row for each participant:
- Name (if provided)
- Leaderboard ranking (for quiz sessions)
- Accuracy (correct, incorrect, and unanswered quiz questions)
- Total submissions across all interactive slide types

**Feedback column** — all written feedback collected during the presentation, plus the overall audience rating score.

### Sharing your report

To share the report with stakeholders who were not in the session, click the **Share** button on the report page and select **Share report via link**. Click **Copy** and send the link. Anyone with the link can view the full presentation report.

## Individual participant report

Click any participant's name in the report to open their individual report. This gives you a detailed breakdown of everything that person submitted during the presentation.

### What the individual report includes

<Accordion title="Participant identification">
  - **Name**, **Email**, and **Organisation** (if provided on the join form or during login). Anonymous participants will not have this information.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Activity summary">
  - **Total Submissions** — across polls, word clouds, scales, open-ended, brainstorm, and Q&A
  - **Accuracy** — overall correct-answer rate on quiz questions
  - **Final Score** and **Ranking** — quiz leaderboard data
  - **Participant Reactions** — total reactions sent
  - **Participant Feedback** — any rating or comments submitted
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Detailed response breakdown">
  **For polls, word clouds, scales, open-ended, brainstorm, and Q&A:**
  - Specific responses, word cloud contributions, scale ratings, open-ended answers, brainstorm ideas, and Q&A questions asked and votes cast

  **For quiz questions:**
  - The participant's answer for each question
  - The correct answer
  - Answer status: **Correct**, **Not Correct**, or **Not Submitted**
  - Response time
  - Points earned (personal points and team contribution points if applicable)
</Accordion>

## Session history

Every time someone resets the presentation's results, AhaSlides creates a new session entry in the report. This means you can review data from past sessions even after running the presentation multiple times.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the report">
    Click the **Report** button in the editor toolbar or hover over the presentation on the dashboard and click **Report**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Session list">
    Click the **Session list** box next to the owner's name on the report page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a session">
    A window lists all recorded sessions. Click any session to view its report.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you use the **Presenter role** for collaborators, each collaborator can only access reports for the sessions they ran themselves. As the owner, you can access all sessions.
</Note>

## Resetting results

Resetting results clears all audience submissions from your presentation — votes, answers, reactions, word cloud entries, and leaderboard data — and returns it to the state it was in before anyone joined. Your slide content (questions, options, settings) is not affected. Each reset creates a new session in the report.

### Reset the entire presentation

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="From the editor">
    Click the **Reset results** button below the slide canvas in the editor.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From the dashboard">
    On the **My Presentations** page, click the **3-dot icon** next to your presentation (or right-click it) and choose **Reset results**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Reset a single slide

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="While editing">
    Right-click the slide in the left navigation panel, then click **Reset results**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="While presenting">
    Hover over the **Menu** icon during the presentation, then click **Reset this slide**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Resetting results is permanent. Audience responses cannot be recovered after a reset. Download your Excel export before resetting if you want to keep a record of the data.
</Warning>

## Collecting audience information

Before participants join your presentation, you can ask them to provide their name, email, organisation, and any custom information you need. This data is stored alongside their responses in the Excel export.

<Note>
  Collecting audience information is available on all **paid plans**. The Excel export with participant info is available to all users not on the Free plan.
</Note>

### Setting up a join form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click the **Settings** menu in the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Collect audience info">
    Click **Collect audience info**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the join form">
    Check **Ask participants for info before they join**. Write a heading for the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add fields">
    Click **Add fields** to choose which information to collect. You can add up to **8 fields** in total: 3 default fields and up to 5 custom fields.

    | Field type | Details |
    |---|---|
    | **Default fields** | Name, Email, Avatar — cannot be renamed, but you can edit the label participants see |
    | **Custom fields** | Set an internal field name (for your Excel export), a question shown to participants, and whether the field is required |
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Collecting info on a specific slide

You can also collect audience information on individual **Open Ended** or **Brainstorm** slides, instead of (or in addition to) the join form.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Edit an Open Ended or Brainstorm slide">
    Open the slide in the editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Collect audience info">
    Click the button next to **Collect audience info** and select the field you want to collect from participants when they answer that slide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Accessing collected data

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Results tab">
    In the editor toolbar, click **Results**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request the Excel file">
    Click **Request Excel file**. AhaSlides generates and downloads the file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Participants sheet">
    In the downloaded Excel file, click the second sheet labelled **Participants** to see all collected participant information alongside their responses.
  </Step>
</Steps>
