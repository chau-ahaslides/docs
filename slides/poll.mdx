A Poll slide is one of the fastest ways to capture live opinions from your audience. You ask a question, provide up to 30 answer options, and watch the results update on screen as participants vote from their phones. No clickers, no paper — just instant, visual feedback.

## How a Poll slide works

When you present a Poll slide, participants tap their preferred option on their own device. The chart on the presenter screen updates in real time as votes come in. Once a participant submits, they also see the current results on their own screen — so everyone stays engaged even after voting.

## Create a Poll slide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a new slide">
    In the AhaSlides editor, click **+ New slide** in the top-left corner. On a brand-new presentation, you can also click **Choose a slide** directly on the canvas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Poll">
    Choose **Poll** from the slide type list. You'll find it under the **Unscored** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your question">
    Type your question in the **Your question** box on the right-hand panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add answer options">
    Fill in your answer options — you can add between **2 and 30** choices. You can also upload an image for each option if you want a visual poll.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview before going live">
    Click **Preview** in the top-right corner to see both the presenter screen and the participant screen side by side before presenting to your audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Present">
    When your slide is ready, click **Present** in the top-right corner. Share the join link or QR code so participants can vote.
  </Step>
</Steps>

That's everything you need for a working Poll slide. All the settings below are optional enhancements.

## Poll settings

<Accordion title="Hide results">
  Keep the results hidden on both the presenter screen and participants' devices until you choose to reveal them. Use this for dramatic reveals or when you don't want early voters to influence later voters.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Show results on audience's phones">
  Allow participants to see everyone's votes on their own device after they've submitted. When this is off, participants see a confirmation screen instead of the live chart.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Show results as percentages">
  Display vote counts as percentages instead of raw numbers. You can also toggle this on the fly during a presentation by clicking any coloured segment on the chart.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Chart layout">
  Choose how results are displayed. Pick from three chart types:

  - **Bar chart** — side-by-side horizontal bars, best for comparing many options.
  - **Donut chart** — a ring chart, clearest when comparing 2–5 options.
  - **Pie chart** — a classic slice chart, good for showing proportional shares.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Allow picking more than one option">
  Let participants select multiple answer options in a single vote. When enabled, set the maximum number of options each person can pick — from 2 up to the total number of options on the slide.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="This question has correct answer(s)">
  Mark one or more options as correct and reveal the answer at a time of your choosing during the presentation. The correct answer stays hidden until you press the **Show correct answer** button on your presenter screen.

  <Note>
    A Poll slide with a correct answer is **not scored** — participants don't earn points. To run a scored quiz where participants compete on a leaderboard, use a slide from the **Quiz** section instead.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Close submission">
  Manually block new votes on this slide. Useful when you want to explain a question before participants can answer, or when you're ready to discuss results and don't want late votes changing the chart.

  While presenting, click the **Submission closed** icon to open voting, and click **Submission opened** to close it again.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Limit time to answer">
  Set a countdown timer between **5 seconds and 20 minutes**. When the timer runs out, the slide stays on screen but no new votes are accepted.

  <Note>
    You can turn the timer off during a live session, but you can't turn it back on without returning to the editor.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

## Tips for great polls

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Keep options distinct" icon="list-check">
    Overlapping options split the vote unpredictably. Make each choice clearly different so results are meaningful.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use images for visual topics" icon="image">
    Upload a photo for each option when you're polling on design, products, or anything visual — it makes options instantly recognisable.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use percentages for large audiences" icon="percent">
    Swap to percentage view when you have 50+ participants. Raw vote counts can feel overwhelming in a large session.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Run self-paced polls" icon="house-laptop">
    For surveys that don't need a live host, enable **Self-paced mode** so participants can vote at their own convenience without you presenting.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
