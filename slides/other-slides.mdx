## Brainstorm

The Brainstorm slide runs a structured three-stage ideation process with your entire audience simultaneously.

**Stages:**
1. **Submit** — Participants type their ideas (up to **200 ideas** total per session). All submissions are hidden from other participants during this stage so ideas aren't anchored to each other.
2. **Vote** — All submitted ideas are revealed and participants vote on their favourites. Each participant has a limited number of votes to allocate.
3. **Results** — Ideas are ranked by vote count, surfacing the group's top choices instantly.

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Brainstorm**.
2. Enter your prompt or question.
3. Set the number of ideas allowed per participant and the votes available per person.
4. Present and guide participants through each stage using the stage controls on your presenter screen.

Brainstorm is ideal for product ideation, retrospective action items, workshop prioritisation, and any situation where you need the group's best ideas ranked by collective preference.

## Rating Scale

The Rating Scale slide lets you ask up to **8 statements** and have participants rate each one on a numeric scale you define.

Participants see each statement and drag a slider (or tap a number) to indicate their rating. Results display as a colour-coded comparative graph — one row per statement — so you can see how the group scored each item side by side.

**Common uses:**
- Post-event feedback (rate each session element from 1–10)
- Net Promoter Score-style questions (likelihood to recommend)
- Multi-criteria product evaluation
- Self-assessment exercises

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Rating Scale**.
2. Type each statement you want participants to rate.
3. Set the minimum and maximum values of the scale and add labels (e.g. *"Strongly Disagree"* to *"Strongly Agree"*).
4. Present — results update live as participants submit.

## Ranking

The Ranking slide gives participants a list of up to **10 options** and asks them to arrange the options in order of preference or importance by dragging them into their chosen sequence.

Results are calculated using **Borda count scoring**: the option ranked first by a participant earns the most points, and each lower rank earns progressively fewer points. The aggregate scores across all participants produce the group's overall ranked order.

**Common uses:**
- Prioritising a backlog of features or projects
- Voting on the order of agenda items
- Choosing a preferred approach from a shortlist
- Team-building preference exercises

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Ranking**.
2. Enter each option participants will be ranking.
3. Present — participants drag the items into their preferred order and submit.

## Pin on Image

The Pin on Image slide lets you upload any custom image and ask participants to place a pin on it. All pins from all participants appear as a **live heat map** as they are submitted.

**Common uses:**
- Spatial feedback (e.g. *"Where on this floor plan would you place the coffee station?"*)
- Annotating diagrams (e.g. *"Click the part of the process where you see the most friction"*)
- Voting on regions of a visual (e.g. *"Which area of this design catches your eye first?"*)
- Geographic targeting (e.g. pinning a map)

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Pin on Image**.
2. Upload your image in the slide editor.
3. Add your question or instruction prompt.
4. Present — participants tap their device screen to place a pin. The heat map on your presenter screen updates live.

## Idea Board

The Idea Board is a collaborative canvas where participants post ideas as individual cards, similar to a digital sticky-note wall.

**How it works:**
- Participants type their idea and submit it as a card on the shared board.
- Cards can be grouped manually by the presenter — drag cards into clusters and label each cluster.
- Enable **AI Grouping** to let AhaSlides cluster similar ideas automatically using semantic analysis.
- After ideas are posted, run a **Voting stage** where participants allocate votes to their favourite cards.

**Common uses:**
- Sprint retrospectives (What went well? What could improve?)
- Workshop brainstorming with visual organisation
- Collaborative agenda building
- Team feedback and recognition boards

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Idea Board**.
2. Add column headers or category labels if you want a structured board (e.g. Start / Stop / Continue).
3. Present — participants submit cards in real time. Use the grouping and voting controls to run subsequent stages.

## Diagram

The Diagram slide creates a visual diagram within your AhaSlides deck — no external tool required. It is a **non-interactive display slide** used to present structured visuals alongside your interactive slides.

**Available diagram types:**

| Type | Best for |
|---|---|
| Relation | Showing connections between concepts |
| Hierarchy | Org charts and reporting structures |
| Items | Simple labelled lists or feature breakdowns |
| Chart | Bar, line, or data chart from manual input |
| Word Cloud | Static word cloud from manually entered words |
| Compare | Side-by-side comparisons of two options |
| Advanced | Custom node-and-edge diagrams for complex relationships |

**How to set it up:**
1. Click **+** and select **Diagram**.
2. Choose a diagram type from the seven available layouts.
3. Enter your content into the diagram builder.
4. Present — the diagram displays as a full-screen visual on your presenter screen.

## Content

The Content slide is a **non-interactive slide** for displaying text, images, GIFs, emojis, and shapes — the equivalent of a standard presentation slide inside AhaSlides.

Use Content slides to:
- Introduce a section before an interactive slide
- Display instructions, rules, or context for a quiz or poll
- Show a closing summary, thank-you message, or call to action
- Include a branded title slide or agenda overview

Content slides support rich formatting including multiple text blocks, layered images, and shapes. They fit seamlessly between interactive slide types so your audience transitions naturally between content and participation.

<Note>
  The Content slide requires a minimum display width of **1,180 px** to render correctly. On screens narrower than this threshold, layout elements may not display as designed. Ensure your presentation display meets this minimum width before presenting.
</Note>
