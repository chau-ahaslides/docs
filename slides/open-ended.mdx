An Open Ended slide lets your audience respond in their own words, with no fixed options to choose from. Responses appear on a live wall on your presenter screen as they arrive, giving you real, unfiltered feedback from the room. Unlike a poll that constrains opinion to pre-set choices, an Open Ended question captures what people actually think — in their own voice.

## How the Open Ended slide works

Participants join on their devices, type their answer in a free-text field, and submit. Each response appears on your presenter screen in real time. You can display responses in a grid (all at once) or reveal them one at a time. You can also delete any response by clicking the **×** next to it during the live session.

## Set up an Open Ended slide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the slide">
    In the editor, click **+ New slide** and select **Open Ended** from the slide type picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your question">
    Type your question in the **Your question** field. Specific questions get better answers — *"What's one thing you'd improve about our onboarding?"* works better than *"What do you think?"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a display layout">
    Under **Results → Layout**, pick how submitted responses are shown on your presenter screen:
    - **Grid** — all responses appear at once as they come in. Good for spotting patterns across a large audience.
    - **One by one** — responses are revealed individually. Useful when you want to read each answer aloud or discuss it with the room.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust settings">
    Configure the optional settings in the right panel to match your session needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Present">
    Click **Present** and share the join link or QR code. Responses appear on your screen as participants submit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Settings

<Accordion title="Time limit">
  Set a countdown timer between **5 seconds and 20 minutes**. When the timer runs out, no new responses are accepted.

  <Note>Available on Edu Medium, Edu Large, Essential, Pro, and Enterprise plans.</Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Close submission">
  Manually block new responses while presenting. Use this once everyone has answered and you're ready to discuss, without advancing to the next slide.

  <Note>Available on Edu Medium, Edu Large, Essential, Pro, and Enterprise plans.</Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Hide results">
  Keeps submitted responses hidden on your screen until you choose to reveal them. Useful for moderated discussions where you don't want responses influencing each other.

  <Note>Available on Edu Large, Pro, and Enterprise plans.</Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Allow audience to submit more than once">
  Lets each participant submit multiple responses — there's no cap on how many times they can contribute.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Allow audience to submit images">
  Participants can share a photo instead of (or alongside) typed text. Useful for show-and-tell activities, design feedback, or any visual context.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Filter profanity">
  Automatically censors English-language swear words in responses. Enabling this applies the profanity filter across your **entire presentation**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Collect audience info">
  Add fields such as name or team before the response box so you can see who said what alongside each submission. Skip this if you've already enabled audience info collection in your presentation-level settings.
</Accordion>

## Smart grouping

When your slide has received **more than 10 submissions**, AhaSlides can automatically cluster similar responses into themes — helping you find patterns without reading every line.

To group responses while presenting:

1. Wait until most participants have submitted.
2. Click the **Group** icon at the bottom of the presenter screen.
3. Click it again to ungroup.

<Warning>
  Smart Grouping works **once per session per slide**. Use it after everyone has submitted — you can't regroup a second time in the same live session.
</Warning>

## Common use cases

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Feedback collection" icon="comment-dots">
    Ask *"What's one thing we should change?"* after a training, event, or sprint review. Free-text responses surface specific, actionable feedback you'd never get from a poll.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Icebreakers" icon="fire">
    Open a session with *"Share one word that describes your week"* or *"What are you hoping to leave with today?"* Seeing everyone's answers on screen warms up the room.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Brainstorming" icon="lightbulb">
    Use Open Ended as a first-pass idea collection before moving to a vote or discussion. The response wall gives everyone a voice, including quieter participants.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Audience reflection" icon="brain">
    In training or education settings, ask *"In your own words, explain…"* to check comprehension without a scored quiz. Responses show you where the gaps are.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  For a more structured brainstorm with voting, consider the **Brainstorm** or **Idea Board** slides — they add a voting stage on top of the open response collection. See [Other Slides](/slides/other-slides) for details.
</Tip>
