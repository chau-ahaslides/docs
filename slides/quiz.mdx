A quiz on AhaSlides is a live competition where participants answer scored questions on their phones and climb a leaderboard. You build the quiz in the editor using one or more of the five quiz slide types, then present it while your audience competes in real time. Whether you're running a classroom review, a corporate training check, or a trivia night, every quiz works the same way: question slides, points, and a winner.

## The five quiz slide types

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pick Answer" icon="list-check" href="/slides/other-slides#pick-answer">
    Classic multiple-choice with up to 8 options. Mark one or more as correct. Supports partial scoring, hints, and image answers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Type Answer" icon="keyboard" href="/slides/other-slides#type-answer">
    No options — participants type their answer. You define correct answers and accepted alternatives. Harder to guess, truer test of knowledge.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Match Pairs" icon="link" href="/slides/other-slides#match-pairs">
    Two columns of up to 4 items. Participants drag to match each prompt on the left to its answer on the right. Supports partial scoring.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Correct Order" icon="arrow-down-1-9" href="/slides/other-slides#correct-order">
    Up to 7 statements shown in a shuffled order. Participants drag them into the sequence you defined. Full correctness required to score.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Categorise" icon="folder-tree" href="/slides/other-slides#categorise">
    Items that must be sorted into up to 8 predefined category buckets. Supports partial scoring. Great for classification and sorting tasks.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The Spinner Wheel appears in the slide type list alongside quiz slides but is **not a scored quiz slide**. It does not award points or appear on the leaderboard.
</Note>

## How scoring works

Every quiz slide has its own point settings. You set a **maximum** and **minimum** point value for each question.

- If **Faster answers get more points** is **off**, every correct answer earns the maximum points.
- If **Faster answers get more points** is **on**, points scale with how quickly a participant answers. For example, with a 100-second timer and 100 max / 0 min points, a player who answers with 80 seconds remaining earns 80 points.

### Partial scoring

On **Pick Answer**, **Match Pairs**, and **Categorise** slides you can enable partial scoring:

- **On** — participants score points for each correct answer or correctly matched item, but any incorrect selection results in 0 points.
- **Off** — participants must get every answer right to score any points.

## Create a quiz: step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add a quiz slide">
    In the editor, click **+ New slide** and select any of the five quiz slide types from the **Quiz** section of the picker. A leaderboard slide is automatically added after it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your question and answers">
    Type your question in the **Your question** field. Then fill in the answer options (or statements, or pairs, depending on the slide type) and mark the correct answer(s).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure scoring and timing">
    Scroll down in the right panel to set the point values, toggle **Faster answers get more points**, and set a time limit. Adjust these per question — they don't need to match across slides.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add more questions">
    Repeat steps 1–3 for each question. Rearrange slides by dragging them in the left panel. Delete leaderboard slides between questions if you only want one final leaderboard at the end.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite players">
    Click **Present**. Players join the quiz lobby using the URL code or QR code shown on your screen, then enter their name and choose an avatar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the quiz">
    Once your players appear in the lobby, click **Start the Quiz!** to begin. Progress through each question, then reveal the final leaderboard when all questions are done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Test your quiz before going live by using **Preview mode** — it lets you play through the quiz as both presenter and participant without needing extra devices.
</Tip>

## Leaderboard

A leaderboard slide is added automatically after each quiz slide you create. It shows the top 5 participants, and the presenter can scroll to see everyone. Participants can see their own rank on their devices.

**The leaderboard does not update in real time.** It fetches the latest results only when you navigate to it or refresh it.

To show the leaderboard just once at the end of a round, delete all the intermediate leaderboard slides and keep only the final one. To restore a deleted leaderboard, open the **Content** tab of the relevant quiz slide and toggle **Leaderboard** back on.

You can also open a **pop-up leaderboard** at any moment without changing the audience screen: press **L** on your keyboard or click the **Trophy icon** in the control bar.

## General quiz settings

Find these in **Settings → General quiz settings** in the right menu bar.

<Accordion title="Sound effects">
  Plays music and sound effects automatically in the quiz lobby, on quiz slides, and on each leaderboard screen. Toggle it on or off directly on screen during a live session.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="5-second countdown">
  Adds a five-second countdown before each question starts, giving participants time to read before the timer begins.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Shuffle options">
  Randomly shuffles answer options for every participant on all Pick Answer, Match Pairs, and Correct Order slides. Makes it harder for participants to share answers by showing each other their screens.

  <Note>Available on Edu Medium, Edu Large, Pro, and Enterprise plans.</Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Manually show correct answers">
  After each question ends, holds the correct answer reveal until you press a button — giving you time to explain or discuss before showing who got it right.

  <Note>Available on all paid plans. Does not work in self-paced mode.</Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Participant avatars">
  Controls whether participants are asked to choose an avatar when they join the quiz lobby. Disable this to let players join with a name only.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Live chat">
  Opens a chat window in the quiz lobby so you and your participants can exchange messages and build atmosphere before the quiz starts.
</Accordion>

## Team quiz

Players can compete in teams instead of individually. Set up teams in **Settings → General quiz settings → Play as team**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn on Play as team">
    Click the **Play as team** switch, then click **Set up**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set team count and size">
    Define how many teams will play and the maximum number of players per team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a scoring rule">
    Pick one of three rules:
    - **Average score of all members** — includes zeros for non-answers; best for equal-sized teams.
    - **Total score of all members** — adds every member's score together, which handicaps teams with fewer members.
    - **Fastest answer only** — registers only the fastest response from each team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your teams">
    Enter a name for each team. Players select their team from this list when they join.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Team quiz is available on Edu Medium, Edu Large, Pro, and Enterprise plans.
</Note>

## Adding hints

On **Pick Answer** slides, you can add up to 3 hints to help players along. Only you as the presenter can choose to reveal a hint during a live question — hints won't appear automatically.

1. Click **Add hint** above the question field.
2. Write up to 3 hints in the boxes.
3. While presenting, press the arrow to reveal each hint one by one.

## Frequently asked questions

<Accordion title="Why isn't my quiz asking players for their names?">
  This usually means you've added a **Poll** slide instead of a **Pick Answer** slide — they look similar but only Pick Answer (and other quiz slides) prompt players for a name. It can also happen if players join while you're on a non-quiz slide; names are only requested when you present a quiz slide.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I clear player answers to reuse a quiz?">
  Go to the presentation report or use the **Reset results** option in the editor. You can clear all responses at once or clear just one question's responses.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How does the 'everyone has answered' auto-end feature work?">
  AhaSlides counts the highest number of participants who were in the quiz simultaneously. If a player leaves after the peak count is set, the timer won't auto-end until all original participants have answered — or you manually end the timer by hovering over it and clicking **End now**.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can players complete the quiz without a live host?">
  Yes. Enable **Self-paced mode** in the presentation settings to let players work through the quiz on their own time, without you presenting it live.
</Accordion>
