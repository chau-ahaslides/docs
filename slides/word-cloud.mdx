A Word Cloud slide invites participants to submit short written responses to a question, then displays every submission together as a cloud where the most popular word grows the largest and sits at the centre. The cloud builds live as responses arrive, with each word rendered in a random colour and shifting position with every update. It's one of the most visually engaging ways to take the pulse of a room instantly.

## How the Word Cloud works

You pose a question and each participant types up to their allowed number of words from their phone. As submissions come in, the cloud on your presenter screen grows in real time. The word submitted most often becomes the largest and moves to the centre. If you delete the most popular word during the session, the next most common word takes its place.

<Note>
  The Word Cloud slide supports up to **5,000 total submissions**, but for the best visual experience approximately 200 words can appear on screen at once.
</Note>

## Set up a Word Cloud slide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the slide">
    In the editor, click **+ New slide** and select **Word Cloud** from the slide type picker. You'll find it in the **Interactive** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your question">
    Type your prompt in the **Your question** field. Short, open-ended questions work best — for example, *"Describe today's session in one word"* or *"What's your biggest challenge right now?"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set entries per participant">
    In the right panel, set how many words each participant can submit — anywhere from **1 to 10**. The default of 3 gives everyone enough voice without flooding the cloud.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure optional settings">
    Adjust the additional settings below to suit your session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview, then present">
    Click **Preview** to see the participant view before going live. When you're ready, click **Present** and share the join link or QR code.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Settings

<Accordion title="Entries per participant">
  Controls how many words each participant can submit, from **1 to 10**. Higher values give more data but can make the cloud noisy.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Time limit">
  Toggle this on to add a countdown timer between **5 seconds and 20 minutes**. When the timer runs out, no new submissions are accepted.

  <Note>
    You can turn the timer off during a live session, but you cannot turn it back on without returning to the editor.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Close submission">
  Manually pause incoming responses without ending the slide. Useful if you need to clarify the question or wait for latecomers. Toggle it back off to reopen submissions.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Hide results">
  Keeps all submitted words hidden on your presenter screen until you choose to reveal them. Perfect for a dramatic moment when you show the full cloud at once.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Allow audience to submit more than once">
  When enabled, participants can keep submitting new words after their initial entries — there's no cap on the total number of submissions from one person.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Filter profanity">
  Automatically censors English-language swear words as they arrive. Enabling this applies the profanity filter across your **entire presentation**, not just this slide.
</Accordion>

## Smart grouping

Smart Grouping clusters words with similar meanings together so you can see dominant themes at a glance — instead of *"happy"*, *"glad"*, and *"content"* appearing separately, they group into one cluster.

To use it while presenting:

1. Scroll to the bottom of the presenter screen.
2. Click the **Group** icon to group similar words instantly.
3. Click the icon again to ungroup and return to the default view.

<Tip>
  Use Smart Grouping after the cloud has stopped building — grouping works best once all (or most) submissions are in.
</Tip>

## Deleting words during a live session

You can remove any individual word from the cloud while presenting by clicking directly on it. This is useful for removing irrelevant or off-topic submissions on the fly, without stopping the session.

## Tips for better Word Clouds

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ask for single words or short phrases" icon="text-size">
    Multi-word sentences break the visual. Prompt participants with *"in one word"* or *"in two words"* to keep the cloud clean and readable.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Set entries per participant to 3–5" icon="sliders">
    A handful of submissions per person builds a rich cloud without any one participant dominating. Use 1 entry per person when you want strict, one-word answers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Hide results for reveals" icon="eye-slash">
    Turn on **Hide results**, collect all responses, then reveal the full cloud at once for a more impactful moment.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Try the Word Cloud → Spinner Wheel workflow" icon="shuffle">
    After the cloud builds, change the slide type to **Spinner Wheel** — every submission automatically becomes a wheel entry. Great for picking a random winner from participant responses.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
